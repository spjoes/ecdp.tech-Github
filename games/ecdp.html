eCDP


eCDP was never released to the public in any form but the game was confirmed to be in the hands of a person under the username of Coddy Trentuit. Due to legal reasons Coddy refused to share the game's files online and the game was considered lost media until November 17, 2020 (11/17/2020) when a Youtuber named Nick Robinson published a video titled "The 10-Year Hunt for the Lost McDonald's DS Game" (which can be found here) where he went over the history of the lost game and then revealed how he got his hands on it. Afterwards he published a full play through of the game (which can be found here) and also dumped the game's rom file which allowed everyone to enjoy and preserve the game.

It is known that about 3,700 stores used eCDP/eSmart with each store being equipped with 2 DS systems for training purposes. The eCDP/eSmart program started in 2009 and seems to have ended in about 2015. Both eSmart and eCDP had an estimated budget of about 200 million yen which is about 2.2mil USD.

As well as a ton of other hidden features, eCDP also contains a master code hidden inside the game which will guarentee access to the game dispite it's set code. This master code was found from a Discord User that goes by the name of "RevoMan". The Mastercode is as follows: "0QKDE9"
